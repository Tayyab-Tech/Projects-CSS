2023 YOUNG FRONTRUNNERS

Because of Them We Can, the global media platform centered on representation, announced the inaugural 2023 'Young Frontrunners' Class today. The 2023 Young Frontrunners Class is a celebration of female Black and Brown culture-shifters across a variety of disciplines, from tech to social justice to fashion and beyond. Because of Them We Can, a platform dedicated to bridging the past, present and future of Black excellence, continues to champion these voices this Women's History Month through its inaugural cohort of women leaders who inspire within their communities and build from the ground up, emerging as powerful forces, making cultural and societal impact from a young age. The 2023 honorees list highlights a number of leaders in their field who are shaping their own future and building for their vision conscientiously.   • MARLEY DIAS

  • Maya Penn

    Nationally Recognized Scientist

  • GABBY GOODWIN

    Creator of GaBBY Bows

  • Anya Dillard

    Founder of The Next Gen Come Up, Activist, Philanthropist, Social Entrepreneur, Content Creator

  • Genesis Butler

    Environmental & Animal Rights Activist

  • NUPOL KIAZOLU

    Activist, Organizer, DEI Strategist and Miss Liberia USA

The list highlights the importance of championing not only Black and Brown voices, but the voices and contributions of young women and supporting existing ecosystems dedicated to building up their communities. This is one of many initiatives of Because of Them We Can, the platform that started out as a 28-day photography campaign and turned into a cultural movement devoted to representation.

“We are honored to unveil our first-ever Young Frontrunners Class honorees. Each of these women is a true inspiration, in the name of culture, community and the future,” said Founder, Eunique Jones Gibson. “We stand on the shoulders of the giants who have come before us and this class is a nod to those giants. Our goal is to amplify these innovators and give credit where credit is due in hopes to continue our mission to educate and inspire the next generation of leaders.”
